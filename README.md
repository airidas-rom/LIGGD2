# 📐 LIGGD2: The Final CAD Presentation

> **The second iteration of the LIGGD CAD and Engineering Organization Portal.**

---

## 📌 Context
This repository represents the second version of the community portal for **LIGGD** (an open, non-profit CAD community that collaborates with national teams globally). It was built after scrapping the first internal draft ([LIGGD1](https://github.com/airidas-rom/LIGGD1)).

Unlike the first iteration, this version was actively pitched and presented to the real client.

## 🛠️ Key Architectural Inclusions
This version of the application was significantly more feature-dense to accommodate the client's operational requirements:
- **Admin Dashboard Integration**
- **Dynamic Content Upload & Removal Framework** (For tracking organizational projects)
- **Digital Book Libraries** (Storage and database tracking for CAD manuals)
- **Blogging & Event Feeds** (For tracking past event outcomes and advertising upcoming community events)

## 🛑 Why it failed (The Design & Pricing Collapse)

Despite the backend structure being robust, the presentation failed fundamentally on two specific axes: **Design** and **Pricing**.

### The Design Disconnect
We explicitly sampled the color palette directly from the client's existing website. The translation from their legacy site to our modern React framework resulted in an aesthetic that felt incredibly amateur and childish. 
It absolutely did not carry the professional weight required for a serious CAD community operating on a national scale. 

Both the client and I internally agreed that it looked poor. My studio partner, however, defended the aesthetic. 
**The Lesson:** *Sometimes your own instinct is right, and you shouldn't blindly defer to the opinions of others just because they are partnered with you.*

### The Pricing Disconnect
Despite recognizing the aesthetic failure, we offered to completely rebuild the platform. However, as part of the overarching trajectory of the Roswaldi Studio's decline, we quoted an absurdly inaccessible price: **1.2k Euros.**

The client instantly rejected the proposal. They reiterated that they were a non-profit organization operating on tight constraints (though realistically, handling national teams implies underlying budget presence). They immediately countered that an external individual had offered to complete the entire stack for **600 Euros**. 

The negotiation ceased, the contract was lost, and Roswaldi Studio formally sunsetted shortly after.
